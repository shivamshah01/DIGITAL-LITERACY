# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is it?

UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where a person is tricked into authorizing a payment for a
fraudulent transaction by a criminal. It is one of the fastest-evolving financial crimes in
India, targeting people of all ages—students, working professionals, and even senior citizens.

---

## How it Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1 — The Setup:**
The victim posts something for sale on an online platform (for example, OLX). The
fraudster contacts the victim saying he/she wants to buy the item.

**Step 2 — Building Trust:**
The fraudster claims to be an Army officer or some other professional and assures the
victim that he/she is willing to buy the item at the full price without any discount.

**Step 3 — The Trick:**
The fraudster then sends a payment request through a UPI app (Google Pay,
PhonePe, Paytm), claiming that this is how payment is made. The victim,
not knowing the difference between a payment request and a payment
confirmation, approves the payment request—unknowingly authorizing a
payment from their own account.

**Step 4 — The Escape:**
The fraudster immediately blocks the victim and becomes unreachable.

---

## Who is Usually Targeted?

First-time online sellers, college students selling old electronics or old
books, and people who are not familiar with how payment requests work
in UPI.

---

## Consequences

The victim loses money instantly. There is no mechanism to get money back.
Since UPI transactions cannot be reversed, banks cannot do anything
to help the victim. In addition to money loss, victims also experience
mental distress and loss of trust in online platforms.
