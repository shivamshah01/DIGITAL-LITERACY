# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Cyber Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and
      symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media,
      and banking apps.

- [ ] **Never click on suspicious links** received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email, even if they appear
      to come from a bank, government body, or known contact.

- [ ] **Verify before you trust** — if someone calls you claiming to be from your bank or a government
      agency and asks for OTP or account details, hang up the call and call the official number directly.

- [ ] **Keep your apps and phone OS updated** — security patches fix vulnerabilities that fraudsters
      actively exploit.

- [ ] **Avoid using public Wi-Fi** for banking, UPI transactions, or logging into sensitive accounts.
      Use mobile data or a trusted VPN instead.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety (Mandatory)

- [ ] **Understand the difference between 'Send Money' and 'Request Money'** in UPI apps.
  Approving a request means money is leaving the account. A genuine buyer won't ask you to
  approve a request. A buyer will send you money.

- [ ] **Do not share your UPI PIN, OTP, or QR code** with anyone, even if they claim to be
  sending you money. A genuine transaction doesn't need you to enter the PIN to receive
  money.

## Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Do not post personal details** such as phone numbers, home address, Aadhaar numbers,
  or daily routine on social media.

- [ ] **Check and adjust privacy settings** for Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat. Make
  them private if you do not need to have them public.

## Report Cybercrime Immediately

If you or someone you know is a victim of cybercrime, please report it immediately.
| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 National Cyber Crime Portal | cybercrime.gov.in |
| 📞 Cyber Crime Helpline | **1930** (available 24x7) |

> Report as soon as possible. The sooner you do it, the higher the chances of freezing
> the fraudulent transactions before the money is withdrawn.
